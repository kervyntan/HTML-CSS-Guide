This page is all about Sharks!

Contrary to popular belief, only a few sharks are dangerous to humans. Out of more than 470 species, only four have been involved in a significant number of fatal, unprovoked attacks on humans: the great white, oceanic whitetip, tiger, and bull sharks.

Types of Sharks

The name 'great white shark' likely comes from the shark's size, as well as the white underside exposed on beached sharks.

Tiger sharks are named for the dark, vertical stripes found mainly on juveniles. As these sharks mature, the lines begin to fade and almost disappear.

The whitetip reef shark is slender and sleek, with characteristic white markings on the tips of several of its fins.

This slender-bodied shark is immediately identifiable by the striking pattern of black saddle-like markings and large spots over its back.
